What is an LDR?

Understand the physics behind light-dependent resistors before touching a single wire.

💡
Key Concept

An LDR (Light Dependent Resistor), also called a photoresistor, is a component whose resistance decreases as light intensity increases.

Core Properties
☀️
In Bright Light
Resistance drops to as low as 1kΩ. Current flows easily through the circuit.
🌙
In Darkness
Resistance rises up to 1MΩ. Current is heavily restricted.
⚡
Photoconductivity
Photons knock electrons loose in semiconductor material, increasing free charge carriers.
🔬
Material: CdS
Usually Cadmium Sulfide. Sensitive to visible light, peaking near 540nm (green-yellow).

Real-World Applications
🌃
Street Lights
Auto ON at dusk, OFF at dawn — no timer needed.
📸
Camera Exposure
Older cameras set shutter time based on scene brightness.
🔆
Screen Brightness
Phones auto-adjust display brightness based on ambient light.
🚨
Alarm Systems
Detect laser beam breaks or sudden light changes.
