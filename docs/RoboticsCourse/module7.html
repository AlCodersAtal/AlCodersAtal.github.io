Chassis Types

The chassis is the skeleton of your robot — it determines how it moves and what it can carry.

🔵2WD Kit2 motors + caster
🟢4WD Kit4 motors, more grip
🟡Tank TracksAll-terrain
🟠OmnidriveSideways movement

For this course we use a 2WD acrylic chassis kit — affordable (under ₹300), easy to assemble, and perfect for an obstacle-avoiding robot.

2WD Differential Steering

  • Both motors forward → drive straight
  • Left backward + Right forward → spin left in place
  • Right backward + Left forward → spin right in place
  • Both backward → reverse
Module 07 · Lesson 2

Power Supply & Battery

Getting power right is the difference between a reliable robot and a frustrating one.

ComponentVoltagePeak Current
Arduino Uno (via VIN)7–12V~50mA
Each TT DC Motor3–6V~400mA stall
L298N motor driver5–35V motor side2A per channel max
HC-SR04 Sensor5V~15mA
SG90 Servo4.8–6V~900mA peak

Recommended Options

  • 4× AA batteries (6V) — Simple, safe, no special charger needed. Great for beginners.
  • 2× 18650 Li-ion in series (7.4V) — Rechargeable, good capacity. Recommended.
  • 2S LiPo (7.4V) — Lightest and most powerful. Requires a dedicated LiPo charger.
⚠️ LiPo Safety
Never discharge a LiPo below 3V per cell. Never charge unattended. Store in a LiPo-safe bag. A swollen or damaged LiPo must be disposed of properly — never puncture it.
💡 One Battery For Everything
Connect battery to the L298N 12V and GND pins. The L298N's onboard regulator outputs 5V to power the Arduino too — one battery runs the entire robot.
