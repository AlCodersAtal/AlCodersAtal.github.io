Robots Around Us

Before you build a robot, let's understand what one actually is — and discover how many you already interact with every day.

What is a Robot?

The word robot comes from the Czech word robota, meaning "forced labour." Today, robots are machines that can sense their environment, make decisions, and take actions — often without continuous human input.

ℹ️ Key Idea
A robot doesn't have to look like a human. A factory arm, a Mars rover, and your Roomba vacuum are all robots.

How Robots Work

Every robot, no matter how complex, follows a simple three-step loop: Sense → Think → Act.

The Sense–Think–Act Cycle

This loop is the heartbeat of every autonomous machine. Your robot repeats it hundreds of times per second:

  1. Sense — Collect data from the environment using sensors (distance, light, temperature…)
  2. Think — Process that data using a microcontroller to decide what to do next.
  3. Act — Execute a physical action using actuators (motors, servos, buzzers…)

A Concrete Example

Think of the obstacle-avoiding robot you'll build by Module 9:

  • Sense: Ultrasonic sensor measures 8 cm to a wall ahead.
  • Think: Arduino checks — 8 cm is less than 15 cm threshold → "too close, turn right."
  • Act: Left motor speeds up, right motor reverses — robot spins right.
💡 Key Insight
Your robot doesn't need to be "smart." It just needs to reliably repeat the Sense–Think–Act loop fast enough. Even the Mars Rover runs this same model.

⚠️ Important
The Arduino cannot power motors directly — the current demand would destroy it. That's why we use a separate motor driver (the L298N). You'll learn this fully in Module 6.
